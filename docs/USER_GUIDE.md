## ✦ What is Keepswell?

</div>

Keepswell is a **memory-keeping app** that helps people gather the
stories, photos, and small everyday moments that make up a life. You
can think of it as a private group journal you share with the people
who matter most — without the noise of social media.

You can use Keepswell on your own, or invite family and friends to
contribute alongside you. Everyone responds when it suits them — by
text message, in the iOS app, or on the website — and their memories
appear together in one beautifully organized **memory book**.

> 🤍 &nbsp; **Our promise.** Your journals are private. Your memories belong
> to you and the people you've invited. We don't sell your data, and
> we never will.

## ✦ Journals & templates

</div>

A **journal** is one collection of memories. Most people have a few —
a family journal, a journal for each of their kids, a vacation
journal, a journal for a big upcoming milestone. Each one has its own
prompts, its own contributors, and its own memory book.

When you create a journal you start from a **template**. Templates
set the tone, suggest prompts, and shape what the finished memory
book looks like:

| Template | What it's for |
|---|---|
| 👨‍👩‍👧 **Family** | Warm, multi-generational prompts about traditions, milestones, and everyday life. |
| 👯 **Friends** | Playful, story-driven prompts perfect for a group you've been close to for years. |
| 💕 **Romantic** | Intimate prompts for couples about shared moments, gratitude, and the small things. |
| ✈️ **Vacation** | Short, in-the-moment prompts designed to be answered on the go, so a trip doesn't fade. |
| 🎉 **Celebration** | Built for milestones — weddings, retirements, graduations — with prompts that invite guests to share their favorite memories. |
| 📔 **Custom** | Start blank and write all of your own prompts. Best when you have a very specific use in mind. |

> ✏️ &nbsp; You can change templates, edit any prompt, or add your own
> custom prompts at any time. Templates are a starting point, not a
> cage.

## ✦ Writing prompts

</div>

Prompts are the quiet engine of Keepswell. Each one is a short
question or invitation — _"What's a small thing that made you smile
this week?"_ — designed to make it easy to write something honest in
under a minute.

When you create a journal, Keepswell automatically generates a queue
of upcoming prompts from the template you picked. **You're in full
control of that queue:**

| You can | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Preview** | See the next several prompts that will be sent. |
| **Edit** | Change the wording on any upcoming prompt so it sounds more like you. |
| **Cancel** | Skip a prompt you don't want to send. |
| **Add** | Drop in a custom prompt that matters to your family or moment. |
| **Pause** | Turn prompts off entirely if you'd rather write on your own schedule. |

Prompts arrive on the cadence you choose — **daily, weekly, biweekly,
or monthly** — at a time and day-of-week of your choosing, in your
local timezone.

## ✦ Memories (entries)

</div>

A **memory** (or "entry") is one thing someone shares with the
journal. It can be a written story, one or more photos, or both.
There's no minimum, no formatting to fuss with, no word count to hit.
**Anything you'd want to remember belongs.**

When you respond to a prompt, your memory is automatically attached
to it. You can also add memories without a prompt anytime you have
something you want to capture.

Other people in the journal can **comment** on memories and add
**reactions** — small acknowledgments that say _"I read this, I loved
it"_ without turning the journal into another social feed.

## ✦ Contributors (participants)

</div>

A journal is more meaningful when other people are in it with you.
**Contributors** can read every memory in the journal, add their own,
comment, and react. Each one gets their own profile in the journal
so memories stay credited to whoever shared them.

You can invite people three ways:

1. **By text message.** Enter their phone number and they'll receive
   a friendly invite they can respond to right from their phone.
2. **By join link or QR code.** Share a link (or a code) that opens a
   simple sign-up form. Great for events.
3. **From the iOS app.** Invite people directly from a journal's
   settings screen.

#### How each contributor receives prompts

For every contributor, **you** choose how prompts reach them:

| Channel | Best for |
|---|---|
| 📱 **SMS** | People who don't want to install an app. Prompts arrive as a regular text. |
| 🔔 **In-app** | Contributors who use the Keepswell iOS app. Prompts land in the **Prompts** tab and as a push notification. |
| ✨ **Both** | The best of both worlds — SMS first, also visible in the in-app feed. |

> 👥 &nbsp; Owners can approve, pause, or remove contributors at any
> time from the journal's settings.

## ✦ The Memory Book

</div>

Everything you and your contributors share automatically flows into a
**memory book** for each journal — a beautiful, readable view of
every memory in chronological order, themed to match the journal
type. Family books feel warm; vacation books feel breezy; celebration
books feel formal and timeless.

The memory book is meant to be **read**. It's not a feed. You can
open it any time on the web or in the iOS app and lose an hour
flipping through what your family has shared.

> 📖 &nbsp; **Read it on a big screen.** The memory book looks especially
> lovely on a laptop or TV browser — perfect for sharing around a
> dinner table.

## ✦ Using the website

</div>

The Keepswell website at **[keepswell.com](https://keepswell.com)** is
the full experience — and the best place to manage journals, invite
contributors, browse the memory book on a big screen, and adjust
settings.

#### Signing up

Click **Get Started** in the header. You can create an account with
your email and a password, or sign in with Google. We'll ask you to
agree to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy when you sign up —
that's all the legal we need.

#### Your dashboard

After signing in you land on your **Dashboard**, which shows every
journal you've created. Click one to open it; click **New Journal**
to start another.

#### Creating a journal

On **Create Journal** you'll:

1. **Pick a template.**
2. **Give the journal a title** and an optional description.
3. **Choose whether to include yourself** as a contributor (and add
   your phone number if you'd like prompts by text).
4. **Decide whether to turn on auto-generated prompts.** We recommend
   leaving this on — you can always edit them later.

#### The journal page

Each journal has its own page where you can:

- Browse every memory chronologically
- Add a new memory yourself
- See and manage contributors
- Open the full Memory Book view
- Open settings to edit prompts, schedule, cover image, sharing, and notifications

#### Settings you can adjust

| Setting | What it controls |
|---|---|
| 🖼️ **Cover image** | Pick from our curated set, paste an image URL, or upload your own. |
| 📅 **Schedule** | Frequency, day of week, time, and timezone for prompts. |
| ✍️ **Upcoming prompts** | Preview, edit, cancel, or add custom prompts to the queue. |
| 📚 **Prompt library** | Reorder the pool of prompts the journal can pull from over time. |
| 🔔 **Notifications** | Choose which events you want to be notified about. |
| 🔗 **Sharing** | Generate a public link so people can view the memory book without signing in. |

## ✦ Using the iOS app

</div>

The Keepswell iOS app gives you the same journals and memories,
designed for capturing things on the go. It's a great fit for quick
photos and short voice-of-the-moment entries.

#### Getting the app

Search for **Keepswell** on the App Store and download it. Sign in
with the same account you use on the website — your journals,
contributors, and memories all sync.

#### Tabs

The app has three tabs at the bottom:

| Tab | What it's for |
|---|---|
| 📓 **Journals** | Every journal you own. Tap one to open it. |
| 💬 **Prompts** | Writing prompts waiting for you across all your journals. Tap **Respond** to compose a memory; tap **Dismiss** to clear it. |
| ⚙️ **Settings** | Your profile, subscription, and account. |

#### Capturing a memory

From any journal tap **＋** to add a memory. You can type freely,
attach photos from your camera roll, or take a new photo right from
the app. There's no save button — your memory goes live when you tap
**Share**.

#### Push notifications

With your permission, the app sends a gentle push when:

- A contributor adds a new memory
- Someone comments on or reacts to a memory in your journal
- A new contributor joins a journal you own
- A new writing prompt is ready for you

> 🛎️ &nbsp; You can turn each of these on or off **per journal** in
> journal settings → Notifications.

#### The Memory Book on iOS

Each journal has its own beautifully themed memory book view inside
the app — perfect for handing your phone to a relative and saying
_"look at all this."_

## ✦ Contributing by text (SMS)

</div>

Not everyone wants another app on their phone, and Keepswell honors
that. **Anyone you invite can fully participate in a journal using
nothing but text messages.**

#### How invites work

When you invite someone by phone number, they get a friendly text
from Keepswell letting them know who invited them and which journal
they've been added to.

| Reply | What happens |
|---|---|
| **YES** | They opt in and start receiving prompts. |
| **STOP** | They stop receiving texts from Keepswell, anytime. |
| **HELP** | They get a short help message and a link to support. |

#### Receiving prompts by text

When a prompt is scheduled, contributors who chose SMS receive the
prompt as a regular text message. They can reply with:

- **Text** — turns into a written memory
- **A photo** — attached to the memory
- **Both** — even better

Their reply automatically becomes a memory attached to that prompt.

> 📲 &nbsp; **Why SMS matters.** A grandparent who doesn't want to
> install anything new can still share the story of how they met your
> grandmother — by text — and it lands in your family memory book
> alongside everyone else's.

#### SMS terms

Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies based on
the journal's schedule. **Your mobile information is never sold or
shared with third parties for promotional purposes.** See the full
SMS terms at
[keepswell.com/sms-consent](https://keepswell.com/sms-consent).

## ✦ Notifications & privacy

</div>

#### You're in control of notifications

For each journal, you can independently turn on or off notifications
for:

- 📝 New memories
- 💬 New comments
- 💗 New reactions
- 👋 New contributors joining

Open the journal's settings and find the **Notifications** section.

#### Your journals are private by default

Only **you** and the contributors **you invite** can see what's
inside a journal. We never make a journal public unless you
explicitly turn on sharing.

#### What we do (and don't) do with your data

| ✅ We do | ❌ We don't |
|---|---|
| Store your memories so you and the people you've invited can read them. | Sell your data. Ever. |
| Send the notifications you've opted into. | Share your phone number or email for marketing. |
| Back up your content in secure, replicated cloud storage. | Make journals public unless you explicitly share them. |
| Permanently delete a journal when you tell us to. | Use your photos or stories to train AI without consent. |

For the full details, read our [Privacy Policy](https://keepswell.com/privacy).

## ✦ Frequently asked questions

</div>

<details>
<summary><b>Do my contributors need to download anything?</b></summary>

No. Anyone can participate over SMS — they just need a phone number
that receives text messages. The iOS app is a nice option for people
who want one, but it's never required.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Is Keepswell free?</b></summary>

Yes — you can start a journal and begin capturing memories at no
cost. We offer a Pro plan with extra features like more journals,
more contributors, and SMS prompts. See current pricing at
[keepswell.com/pricing](https://keepswell.com/pricing).

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Can I have more than one journal?</b></summary>

Yes. Many people have a few — one for the family, one for each
child, one for a specific trip or event. Each one is independent
with its own prompts and contributors.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>What if a contributor doesn't want to be in the journal anymore?</b></summary>

They can reply **STOP** to a text to opt out, and the journal owner
can remove anyone from the participants list at any time.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Can I print my memory book?</b></summary>

We're working on print export. Today you can read the full memory
book on the website or in the iOS app, and share the public link
with anyone you want to see it.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>What happens if I delete a journal?</b></summary>

The journal and every memory, photo, and contributor record inside
it is permanently removed. We can't recover a deleted journal — so
we ask you to type the journal's name to confirm before deleting.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Can I edit a memory after I share it?</b></summary>

The person who created a memory can hide or remove it. Owners can
also hide any memory from the memory book.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Is my data backed up?</b></summary>

Yes. Memories and photos are stored in secure, replicated cloud
storage. We take backups seriously — your family stories should
outlast any one app or device.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Can I change a journal's template later?</b></summary>

Yes. You can switch templates from the journal's settings at any
time. The prompts you've already received and the memories you've
already saved stay exactly as they were.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Can I turn off prompts?</b></summary>

Yes — either when you create the journal, or in the settings later.
You can also remove yourself as a contributor on that journal while
keeping the journal active for others.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Does Keepswell work outside the US?</b></summary>

The website and iOS app work worldwide. SMS prompts are currently
optimized for US phone numbers; for non-US numbers we recommend the
in-app or web experience.

</details>
